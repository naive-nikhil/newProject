FAQs on optimizing your AWS costs

AWS offers several pricing options that can dramatically cut costs:

  • EC2 On-Demand Instances for flexible, short-term workloads
  • EC2 Spot Instances for interruption-tolerant jobs (up to 90 % savings)
  • Savings Plans with 1- or 3-year commitments and flexible instance families
  • EC2 Reserved Instances (Standard or Convertible) with up to 75 % discounts and multiple payment terms (all-upfront, partial-upfront, no-upfront)

Stable analyzes your historical usage, forecasts future demand, and recommends the ideal blend of Reserved Instances and Savings Plans. A centralized console then allocates RIs to the right organizational units for maximum return.

Decide case-by-case between managed services such as Amazon RDS and self-managed EC2 deployments, according to your operational expertise and monthly spend (> USD $1,000). For EC2, fine-tune your choice among Graviton 2/3 (ARM), AMD, or Intel instances to match each workload. Consider specialized instance families—compute-optimized, memory-optimized, storage-optimized—for targeted use cases.

Stable enriches AWS Compute Optimizer data with detailed CloudWatch metrics (CPU, memory, IOPS, network throughput) to recommend better-fitting instance types, complete with precise savings estimates.

Idle assets burn money even when they’re doing nothing. Stable automatically flags:

  • ELBs with no registered instances
  • Detached Elastic IPs
  • Oversized NAT Gateways
  • Orphaned EBS volumes
  • RDS read replicas with zero traffic
  • Dormant Lambda functions
  • Redundant EBS/RDS snapshots

Implement a lifecycle-management strategy for your EBS and RDS snapshots. Use granular retention policies (e.g., keep hourly snapshots for 24 h, daily for 7 days, weekly for 1 month, monthly for 1 year). Stable pinpoints redundant, obsolete, or orphaned snapshots and precisely calculates the savings achievable through AWS Data Lifecycle Manager automation. Our solution also detects snapshots eligible for archiving and can orchestrate their transfer to lower-cost tiers.

Detached EBS volumes can account for up to 50 % of your AWS bill. Fine-tune the volume type as well (gp2, gp3, io1, io2, st1, sc1) to match your real IOPS and throughput needs; migrating from gp2 to gp3 alone can trim costs by about 20% at equal performance. Stable analyzes usage metrics for every volume and recommends:

  • Deleting orphaned volumes
  • Converting to the optimal volume type
  • Precisely right-sizing provisioned IOPS
  • Tiering cold data to S3 via AWS DataSync
  • Enabling automatic backups through AWS Backup

Analyze actual utilization in depth (not just CPU) to uncover right-sizing opportunities. Stable collects fine-grained CloudWatch metrics (including memory utilization via the CloudWatch Agent), studies usage patterns (p95, p99), and produces resize recommendations that respect burst needs. Our platform can also model the financial impact of adopting a multi-AZ or multi-region architecture, factoring in all related data-transfer costs.

AWS data-transfer pricing is complex and can swallow a sizeable chunk of your bill. Optimize it by:

  • Strategic use of VPC Endpoints to slash NAT Gateway charges
  • Deploying Amazon CloudFront for caching and lower Internet egress
  • Choosing regional vs. global S3 endpoints to suit each workload
  • Reducing inter-AZ traffic through smarter instance placement
  • Leveraging AWS PrivateLink for service-to-service communication

Go beyond simple horizontal scaling and adopt advanced strategies:

  • Predictive scaling driven by historical usage patterns
  • Mixed-instance strategies that blend On-Demand and Spot capacity
  • Scheduled scaling for workloads you can calendar in advance
  • Step scaling for applications with complex demand curves

Stable refines your Auto Scaling Groups by mining historical performance metrics and dialling in the optimal settings: Min./Max./Desired capacity, cooldown periods, and scale-in protection.

S3 Intelligent-Tiering automates movement between frequent-access, infrequent-access, and archive tiers. To unlock maximum savings:

  • Activate the Archive Access and Deep Archive Access tiers
  • Apply filters based on object tags or prefixes
  • Combine Intelligent-Tiering with Lifecycle policies for permanent moves

Stable reviews every S3 bucket, maps real-world access patterns, and builds an optimal tiering plan, mixing S3 Standard-IA, S3 One Zone-IA, and Glacier classes to fit each data category.

Configure granular scheduling policies:

  • Environment-specific timetables (Dev/Test/QA)
  • Hibernate instead of stopping to preserve RAM state
  • Tag-based custom time windows
  • Exception periods for maintenance or deployments
  • Integration with corporate calendars via API

Stable goes further by automatically spotting the best instances for scheduling based on historical usage patterns and can roll out Instance Scheduler configurations tailored to your organization.

For AWS Lambda functions, granular optimizations are within reach:

  • Migrate to ARM (Graviton2) runtimes for ~20% savings
  • Precisely tune allocated memory based on execution profiling
  • Use Provisioned Concurrency for predictable workloads
  • Implement connection pooling to shrink cold-start times
  • Optimize code placement to reduce initialization latency
  • Leverage Lambda SnapStart for Java runtimes

Stable analyzes every function to pinpoint its ideal configuration and recommends lean, event-driven architectures to replace costlier services.

Cost control isn’t a one-off project. Stable provides:

  • Full cost disaggregation by environment, team, project, and component
  • Machine-learning anomaly detection
  • Budget forecasts and scenario modelling
  • Precise allocation of shared costs
  • Industry benchmark dashboards
  • Real-time alerts that plug into CI/CD to block wasteful deployments before they go live

With Stable’s unified FinOps platform, you get customizable dashboards, always-on anomaly alerts, and cost-aware pipelines—so cloud waste never sneaks back onto your AWS invoice.
